From Birth to 40 Years of Age

The Life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

This educational resource explores the early life and character of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Makkah before the beginning of his prophetic mission. Focusing on the period from his birth in the Year of the Elephant (570 CE) to the reception of the first revelation in the Cave of Hira (610 CE) at the age of forty, the site traces his development as a trusted member of Quraysh society.

The Pre-Prophetic Biography
Introduction

The Early Biography

This digital archive aims to provide a reliable, objective, and scholarly overview of the first forty years of the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Focusing exclusively on this pre-prophetic era allows students and researchers to gain deep insights into his early upbringing, ancestral lineage, and the socio-economic structure of pre-Islamic Arabia.

Understanding these formative years is crucial for historic context, as the noble qualities of honesty, mediation, and wisdom he exhibited from a young age laid the groundwork for his reputation as the most trusted member of Makkah’s society before his calling at the age of forty.

Lineage & Origin

Birth & Historical Background

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in Makkah in the Year of the Elephant, historically estimated around 570 CE. This year was marked by the unsuccessful campaign of Abraha, the governor of Yemen, to destroy the Kaaba, an event that deeply reinforced the sacred character of Makkah throughout Arabia. He belonged to the noble clan of Banu Hashim, a prominent and highly respected lineage within the dominant tribe of Quraysh.

His father, Abdullah, was the son of the Makkan chieftain Abdul Muttalib, and his mother, Aminah, was the daughter of Wahb ibn Abd Manaf from the clan of Banu Zuhrah. Sadly, his father Abdullah passed away during a trading journey to Yathrib (later Madinah) several months before his birth, leaving him to be born as an orphan under the stewardship of his grandfather in the dry valley settlement of Makkah.

570 CE — Makkah
Formative Years

Childhood & Guardianship

Following the traditional custom of Makkan nobility, the infant Muhammad ﷺ was sent to the clean, dry air of the surrounding desert to be raised by a foster family. He was placed under the care of Halimah al-Sa'diyah of the Banu Sa'd clan, with whom he spent his first few years learning the pure Arabic dialect and developing physical resilience. He returned to his mother Aminah at around five years of age.

When he was six, his mother fell ill and passed away at Al-Abwa during their return from a family visit to Yathrib. He was then placed under the custody of his grandfather, Abdul Muttalib, who cherished him deeply. Just two years later, his grandfather also died. At the age of eight, the young boy was taken in by his paternal uncle, Abu Talib, who raised him with great affection alongside his own children.

Desert Upbringing
Al-Amin

Youth & Character Development

Under his uncle Abu Talib's guidance, Muhammad ﷺ began learning the trade business, accompanying caravans to places such as Syria. In his youth, he stood out from Makkah's society through his impeccable behavior, modesty, and complete avoidance of the common social ills of the time, such as drinking and idol worship. He earned a livelihood as a shepherd and later as a commercial agent.

His fair dealings, absolute honesty, and reliability in all trade negotiations earned him the prestigious honorific titles of Al-Amin (The Trustworthy) and As-Sadiq (The Truthful) from the citizens of Makkah. He was widely respected among the people for his peaceful demeanor, truthfulness, and integrity in both commercial and personal matters.

Integrity & Honor
Sacred Partnership

Marriage to Khadijah (RA)

Muhammad’s ﷺ reputation for honesty and business acumen led to a commercial partnership with Khadijah bint Khuwaylid (RA), a wealthy, independent, and highly respected noblewoman and merchant of Makkah. She entrusted him with her trade goods for a major caravan to Syria, accompanied by her servant Maysarah. Upon returning, Maysarah reported Muhammad’s ﷺ exceptional business management, modesty, and outstanding moral integrity.

Impressed by these qualities, Khadijah (RA) proposed marriage through a close friend. Muhammad ﷺ accepted after consulting his uncle Abu Talib. They were married in 595 CE, when he was twenty-five and she was forty. Their union was a source of great mutual support, respect, and deep affection. Khadijah (RA) remained his sole wife and closest advisor during her lifetime, and together they raised several children.

595 CE — Union
Pre-Prophetic Milestones

Major Events Before Prophethood

Key historical events that defined Muhammad’s ﷺ early life and demonstrated his leadership and spiritual character in Makkah.

Justice

Hilf al-Fudul

During his youth, Muhammad ﷺ participated in Hilf al-Fudul (The Pledge of Virtue), an alliance formed by Makkan clans to uphold justice, protect the weak, and ensure foreign traders were not cheated. Throughout his life, he expressed great pride in this covenant of fairness.

Arbitration

Kaaba Reconstruction

At age 35, when a flood damaged the Kaaba, the clans disputed over who would place the Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad) back in its corner. Muhammad ﷺ peacefully resolved the threat of tribal war by placing the stone on a cloak and having all tribal leaders lift it together.

Solitude

Cave of Hira

As he approached forty, Muhammad ﷺ increasingly sought isolation from the social corruption and pagan rituals of Makkah. He began retreating to the Cave of Hira on Mount Light (Jabal al-Nour) to spend days in quiet meditation and contemplation, seeking spiritual peace.

Chronology

Prophetic Timeline

A chronological summary of the major milestones in the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ up to his first revelation at the age of forty.

570 CE

Birth in Makkah

Born in the Year of the Elephant to Aminah and the late Abdullah of the noble Banu Hashim clan.

576 CE

Death of His Mother Aminah

Orphaned of his mother at the age of six during their return journey from Yathrib (Madinah).

578 CE

Death of Abdul Muttalib

Loses his grandfather and guardian, Abdul Muttalib, at age eight. Custody transfers to his uncle Abu Talib.

595 CE

Marriage to Khadijah (RA)

Marries the noblewoman Khadijah (RA) at age twenty-five after successfully managing her trade caravans to Syria.

605 CE

Kaaba Reconstruction

Successfully arbitrates the Black Stone dispute at age thirty-five, preventing conflict between the tribal factions.

610 CE

First Revelation

Receives the first revelation of the Holy Quran through Angel Jibreel in the Cave of Hira, marking the end of his pre-prophetic era.

References & Sources

Key historical references and classical texts used to verify the pre-prophetic biography of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

01

The Holy Quran

Primary Islamic Scripture

The primary sacred scripture of Islam, providing foundational direct references to the Prophet's early life (such as his orphanhood in Surah Ad-Duha) and his pristine character.

02

Sahih al-Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

Hadith Collections

The two most authentic collections of Hadith, preserving detailed historical accounts of his early life, marriage, and the events leading up to the first revelation.

03

Ar-Raheeq Al-Makhtum (The Sealed Nectar)

Written by Sheikh Safiur-Rahman al-Mubarakpuri

A comprehensive, award-winning modern biography providing meticulous chronological details of the Prophet's pre-prophetic years.

04

Seerah Ibn Hisham

Edited by Ibn Hisham

The classical, foundational biography edited by Ibn Hisham, which acts as the primary historical reference for the early years of the Prophet ﷺ.
